ROBOLUTION

Mechanics General Conduct General Rules Participant Requirement Team Requirement

01

Open To All Students

Senior high school and college students are eligible to join the competition. Participants must be able to provide proof of their enrollment status upon request.

02

Register

Participants are expected to register before the designated deadline and must provide all necessary information.

03

Objective

The project to be created is a line-following robot that will race on a line track. The objective for all participants is to build the fastest line-following robot.

04

Restrictions

The competition has no restrictions on the tools, applications, or software that participants may use.

05

The Kit

The robotics kit and line-track will be provided by Erovoutika.

06

Elimination

The competition will be based on the winners per race and will be running a single elimination format. The robots will be paired and will race against each other, losers will be eliminated, winners will advance in the tournament.

07

Sports
manship

By participating, all participants automatically agree that they vow to respect fellow participants and staff.

Players are expected to compete with the spirit of sportsmanship, maintaining a friendly and polite attitude towards other participants.

The organizer/facilitators have the right to apply penalties, disqualify and dismiss any registered participants from the competition at their discretion.

Be on Time

Participants must be at designated venue at least 30 minutes prior to scheduled start time. Latecomers will be disqualified.

Provided Kit

Projects to be submitted for the competition must only be created using the components and provided by the designated robotics kit.

Remain Inside

Participants must remain within the competition area once the event has begun. Participants who will leave during the event will be disqualified.

No Talking

During the event, participating groups must not communicate with each other. Any attempt to do so will lead to disqualification.

Authority

The organizers have the authority to modify the rules as needed and disqualify teams for undisclosed reasons or take alternative measures.

Rules

Not knowing the rules doesn't excuse breaking them. If you have any questions about the rules, please reach out to our support.

Teams will be created either in random order or based on the participants' preferences. All teams must consist of at least 3-4 members. Participants who do not have a team upon registration will be assigned to a team by the organizers.
Each team will be given a team name and must work together throughout the competition. Participants may request to change teams prior to the start of the competition, but the organizers have the authority to deny such requests.

Participants must form up until two (2) members, randomly or selected. Participants with no team upon registration will be assigned to a new one by the organizers. They may also have a maximum of one (1) coach per team.

Changing of teams is permissible; however, the organizers have the right to deny a request. Each team will be given a team name and is expected to work together through the competition.
